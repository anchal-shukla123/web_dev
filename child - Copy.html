Child Labour Rights

Complete guide to child labour laws, protections, and government schemes.

Understanding Child Labour

Child labour deprives children of education, health, and dignity. Around 152 million children worldwide are engaged in labour (ILO Report, 2021). India legally protects children under 14 from employment in hazardous and non-hazardous sectors.

Key Legal Rights for Children in India

  • Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986: Bans employment of children below 14 years.
  • Right to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009: Ensures free education for children aged 6â€“14 years.
  • Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015: Punishes those who employ child labourers.
  • Constitutional Rights: Articles 24, 39(e), and 45 protect against exploitation and promote education.

Major Government Schemes to Combat Child Labour

  • National Child Labour Project (NCLP): Rehabilitation and education of rescued child workers.
  • Mid-Day Meal Scheme: Encourages school enrollment by providing free meals.
  • Operation Smile and Muskaan: Special rescue drives to locate and free child labourers.
  • Bal Swaraj Portal: Online tracking and monitoring of vulnerable children (Visit NCPCR).

Helpline Numbers & Resources

  • Childline: 1098 (24x7 helpline for children in distress)
  • Police Emergency: 112
  • National Commission for Protection of Child Rights: ncpcr.gov.in

Important Court Judgments

  • M.C. Mehta vs. State of Tamil Nadu (1996): Guidelines for regulating child labour and ensuring education.
  • Bachpan Bachao Andolan vs. Union of India (2011): Strengthened mechanisms against child trafficking and labour.
