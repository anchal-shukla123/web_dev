Consumer Rights

Complete guide to consumer rights, protections, services, and support systems for consumers.

Understanding Consumer Rights

Consumer rights ensure that people can buy goods and services with fairness, transparency, and without being exploited. The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 provides protection against unfair trade practices, defective products, and misleading advertisements.

Key Legal Rights for Consumers

  • Right to Safety: Protection from goods and services that are hazardous to health and life.
  • Right to be Informed: Right to receive truthful information about products and services.
  • Right to Choose: Consumers have the right to access a variety of products and services at competitive prices.
  • Right to be Heard: Right to voice complaints and concerns in a fair and transparent manner.
  • Right to Seek Redressal: Right to seek compensation or redressal for defective goods or services.

Major Government Schemes and Services for Consumers

  • Consumer Helpline (1915): A government initiative for consumers to lodge complaints and get assistance on various issues related to goods, services, and consumer rights.
  • National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (NCDRC): Provides a platform for resolving consumer disputes and complaints at the national level.
  • Consumer Protection Act, 2019: The Act ensures protection from unfair trade practices, guarantees consumer rights, and promotes consumer awareness.
  • Consumer Education and Awareness Programme: Government initiatives to spread awareness about consumer rights through various media campaigns.

Common Consumer Issues

  • Unfair Trade Practices: Misleading advertisements, false claims, and aggressive marketing tactics.
  • Defective Goods and Services: Receiving faulty products, poor services, or non-delivery of services promised.
  • Unethical Practices: Products being sold without proper labeling, pricing, and without adequate information regarding their origin or use.
  • Excessive Pricing: Charging for products or services more than their actual value without justification.
