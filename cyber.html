Cyber Security Rights

Complete guide to your rights, protections, schemes, and support systems in the digital space.

Understanding Cyber Security Rights

Cyber security rights refer to the protections, laws, and frameworks that safeguard individuals from online threats such as identity theft, cyberbullying, hacking, and data breaches. These rights ensure individuals have control over their personal data and privacy while interacting online.

Key Legal Rights in Cyber Security

  • Right to Privacy: Right to safeguard personal data and information against unauthorized access and misuse (Article 21 of the Indian Constitution, right to privacy).
  • Right to Data Protection: Protection of personal data under the proposed Personal Data Protection Bill, 2019.
  • Right Against Cyberbullying: Right to report and seek redressal for cyberbullying and online harassment.
  • Right to Digital Security: Right to secure digital environments and systems free from hacking, phishing, and malware attacks.
  • Right to Access Information: Right to access digital information freely while ensuring that security and privacy are not compromised.

Common Cyber Security Issues

  • Identity Theft: When cybercriminals steal personal information like name, address, social security number, or bank details to commit fraud.
  • Phishing: Cybercriminals using deceptive emails or websites to steal sensitive information like login credentials, passwords, or credit card details.
  • Cyberbullying: Harassment or bullying using digital platforms, including social media, emails, or texts.
  • Ransomware: Malware that locks or encrypts your device data and demands payment for the decryption key.
  • Data Breaches: Unauthorized access and exposure of sensitive personal data held by companies, organizations, or government entities.

Important Court Judgments

  • Shreya Singhal vs Union of India (2015): Supreme Court ruling on the unconstitutionality of Section 66A of the IT Act, which restricted freedom of expression online.
  • K.S. Puttaswamy vs Union of India (2017): Recognized the right to privacy as a fundamental right under the Indian Constitution, directly impacting digital privacy and data protection.
