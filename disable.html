Disability Rights

Complete guide to rights, protections, schemes, and support systems for individuals with disabilities.

Understanding Disability Rights

Disability rights include the rights of persons with disabilities to have access to education, healthcare, employment, social services, and protection from discrimination. The Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, is the key legislation ensuring these rights in India.

Key Legal Rights for Persons with Disabilities

  • Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016: Protects the rights of individuals with disabilities to access education, employment, transportation, and more.
  • Reservation in Government Jobs: Persons with disabilities are entitled to a 4% reservation in government jobs.
  • Accessibility Rights: Rights to accessible infrastructure, public spaces, and transportation.
  • Equal Access to Education: Ensures equal access to education with necessary accommodations.

Major Government Schemes for Disabilities

  • Deendayal Disabled Rehabilitation Scheme (DDRS): Provides assistance for the rehabilitation of persons with disabilities.
  • National Handicapped Finance and Development Corporation (NHFDC): Provides financial assistance for disabled persons to set up their own businesses.
  • Assistance to Disabled Persons for Purchase/Fitting of Aids and Appliances (ADIP): Provides financial support to buy assistive devices.
  • National Scholarship for Students with Disabilities: Financial support for higher education.

Common Issues Faced by Persons with Disabilities

  • Lack of Accessibility: Insufficient infrastructure, ramps, elevators, and public transportation options for people with disabilities.
  • Discrimination: People with disabilities often face social exclusion and discrimination in schools, workplaces, and public life.
  • Healthcare Barriers: Limited access to specialized healthcare services, treatments, and rehabilitation.
  • Unemployment: Despite legal protections, many individuals with disabilities remain unemployed or underemployed.

Important Court Judgments

  • R. K. Sabharwal vs. Union of India (1999): Supreme Court ruling on the implementation of disability reservations in public services.
  • SC Directive (2015): Courts have directed all public buildings to make facilities accessible for persons with disabilities.
