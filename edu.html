Educational Rights

Complete guide to your right to education, scholarships, helplines, and educational challenges.

Understanding Educational Rights

Education is a fundamental right ensuring individuals receive free, quality schooling without discrimination. It empowers people to live independently and contribute to society.

Key Educational Rights in India

  • Right to Education (RTE) Act, 2009: Free and compulsory education for children aged 6-14 years.
  • Article 21A (Constitution of India): Guarantees right to education as a fundamental right.
  • Mid-Day Meal Scheme: Nutritious meals for school-going children to improve enrollment and retention.
  • Reservation Policies: Reserved seats for SC/ST/OBC and economically weaker sections.

Major Government Schemes for Education

  • Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan: Unified scheme for school education from pre-primary to Class 12.
  • National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship (NMMS): Financial assistance to meritorious students of economically weaker sections.
  • PM-USP Yojana: Scholarships for university and college students.
  • SWAYAM: Free online courses from top institutions (Visit SWAYAM).

Common Issues in Education Sector

  • Dropout Rates: High dropout rates, especially among girls and rural children.
  • Quality of Education: Lack of trained teachers and infrastructure.
  • Digital Divide: Limited access to online education in remote areas.
  • Affordability: Rising cost of private education institutions.

Important Court Judgments

  • Mohini Jain vs. State of Karnataka (1992): Recognized the right to education as a fundamental right.
  • Unni Krishnan vs. State of Andhra Pradesh (1993): Detailed the scope of the right to education up to the age of 14 years.
