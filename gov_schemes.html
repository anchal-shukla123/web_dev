Government Schemes in India

Comprehensive guide to all government schemes and benefits available for citizens in India.

What are Government Schemes?

Government schemes are various programs initiated by the Indian government to promote welfare, economic growth, and development in different sectors such as education, healthcare, employment, and more. These schemes aim to improve the quality of life for all citizens, particularly the underprivileged.

Key Government Schemes in India

  • Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY): A financial inclusion scheme aiming to provide universal access to banking facilities and financial services.
  • Swachh Bharat Abhiyan: A cleanliness initiative to improve sanitation and eliminate open defecation in rural and urban areas.
  • Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY): Provides free LPG connections to women below the poverty line.
  • Make in India: Aims to encourage national and international companies to manufacture their products in India, boosting the manufacturing sector.
  • Skill India Mission: Focuses on enhancing skills in youth, particularly in vocational and technical training for better job opportunities.
  • Ayushman Bharat Yojana: A health insurance scheme providing free access to healthcare for families in rural areas.

Schemes for Women

  • Beti Bachao Beti Padhao Yojana: Aims to address the declining child sex ratio and promote education for girls.
  • Mahila Shakti Kendra Scheme: Empowers rural women by providing them access to government services and information.
  • Women Helpline Scheme: Provides a toll-free number (181) to women in distress for emergencies and immediate help.
  • Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana: Financial support to pregnant women for maternity benefits.

Schemes for Farmers

  • Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY): Crop insurance scheme to help farmers recover from crop damage due to natural disasters.
  • PM Kisan Yojana: Direct income support for farmers to ensure financial stability.
  • Soil Health Card Scheme: Provides soil health cards to farmers to ensure proper soil management for higher yields.

Schemes for Education

  • Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan: Aims to provide quality education from primary to higher education and promote inclusive education.
  • RTE (Right to Education) Act: Ensures free and compulsory education for children between 6 to 14 years of age.
  • National Means-Cum-Merit Scholarship Scheme: Provides scholarships to students from economically weaker sections of society to pursue education.

Schemes for Healthcare

  • Ayushman Bharat Yojana: Provides health insurance for the poorest families, offering free medical treatment and hospitalization.
  • National Health Mission: Focuses on providing universal health coverage, including maternal health, child health, and family welfare.
  • Jan Aushadhi Yojana: Provides affordable generic medicines through Pradhan Mantri Bhartiya Janaushadhi Kendras.

Helplines for Government Schemes

  • PMJDY Helpline: 1800-11-0000
  • Ayushman Bharat Helpline: 14555
  • Women Helpline (181): For immediate assistance to women in distress.
  • Farmers Helpline: 1800-180-1551 (For queries related to schemes and policies).
  • National Health Mission Helpline: 1800-180-1104 (For health-related queries and scheme details).
