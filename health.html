Health Rights

Complete guide to health rights, free services, key schemes, and health-related helplines.

Understanding Health Rights

Health is a human right, ensuring everyone has access to timely, acceptable, and affordable health care of appropriate quality. Good health is central to human happiness and well-being.

Key Health Rights in India

  • Right to Health: Recognized under Article 21 (Right to Life) of the Indian Constitution.
  • Right to Emergency Medical Care: No hospital can deny emergency treatment (Paschim Banga case, 1996).
  • Right to Information: Patients have the right to know about their illness, treatment options, and costs.
  • Right to Privacy and Confidentiality: Medical information must be kept confidential unless legally required to be shared.

  • Mission Indradhanush: Full immunization program for children under 2 years and pregnant women.

Common Issues in Health Sector

  • Shortage of Healthcare Workers: Especially in rural and remote areas.
  • Out-of-Pocket Expenditure: High medical costs leading to financial burden on families.
  • Access to Mental Health Services: Limited awareness and availability of mental health care.
  • Quality Control: Need for better regulation and quality checks in hospitals and clinics.

Important Court Judgments

  • Paschim Banga Khet Mazdoor Samity vs. State of West Bengal (1996): Supreme Court held the right to timely medical treatment as a part of the right to life.
  • Consumer Protection Act (2019): Strengthened patient rights regarding medical negligence and service deficiencies.
