Senior Citizen Rights

Complete guide to rights, government services, schemes, and helpline support for senior citizens.

Understanding Senior Citizen Rights

Senior citizens have the right to lead a life of dignity, security, and freedom from exploitation. The Indian Constitution guarantees these rights through various policies and welfare schemes.

Key Rights for Senior Citizens in India

  • Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act, 2007: Legal protection ensuring children and relatives provide care and maintenance.
  • Right to Healthcare: Free and subsidized medical services in government hospitals.
  • Right to Concessions: Discounts in railway, air travel, and tax exemptions on income.
  • Right to Protection: Special provisions to safeguard against abuse, neglect, and financial exploitation.

Major Government Schemes for Senior Citizens

  • Varishtha Pension Bima Yojana: Provides social security and financial support through a guaranteed pension.
  • Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension Scheme (IGNOAPS): Financial assistance to elderly persons below the poverty line.
  • Rashtriya Vayoshri Yojana: Free physical aids and assisted-living devices to senior citizens from BPL families.
  • Senior Citizen Health Insurance Scheme: Health coverage at affordable rates for people above 60 years.

Common Issues Faced by Senior Citizens

  • Neglect and Abuse: Physical, emotional, and financial abuse by family or caregivers.
  • Healthcare Accessibility: Lack of age-friendly hospitals and specialized geriatric services.
  • Financial Security: Inadequate pensions and post-retirement support systems.
  • Social Isolation: Loneliness and mental health challenges among the elderly.

Helpline Numbers & Resources

  • Senior Citizen Helpline: 14567
  • Elder Line National Toll-Free Number: 1800-180-1253
  • Police Helpline (Senior Citizen Protection): 1291 (City-specific in some states)
  • HelpAge India: Visit HelpAge India

Important Court Judgments

  • Maintenance under Section 125 CrPC: Parents can claim maintenance from children under criminal law if neglected.
  • Supreme Court Directive (2018): Directed states to set up old age homes in every district.
