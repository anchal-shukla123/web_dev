Voting Rights

Your complete guide to voting rights, protections, schemes, and support systems.

Understanding Voting Rights

Voting is a fundamental right for citizens in a democratic society. In India, voting rights are granted to citizens who are at least 18 years of age, ensuring that they have a say in the democratic processes and governance of the country.

Key Legal Rights for Voting in India

  • Right to Vote: Every citizen above the age of 18 has the right to vote in elections (Article 326 of the Indian Constitution).
  • Right to Universal Suffrage: The right to vote cannot be denied based on race, religion, caste, sex, or economic status.
  • Right to Secrecy: The right to vote in private without fear of coercion or intimidation.
  • Right to Elect Representatives: Every voter has the right to choose their representatives in Lok Sabha and Vidhan Sabha elections.
  • Right to Contest Elections: Citizens are allowed to contest elections if they meet the required qualifications.

Major Government Schemes for Voting Rights

  • National Voters' Service Portal (NVSP): A digital portal for registration, voter ID services, and tracking election details.
  • Voter Helpline App: Provides information about voter registration, polling station locations, and election-related queries.
  • Electoral Roll Revision: The process through which eligible citizens can ensure their names are added to the electoral rolls.
  • Booth Level Officers (BLOs): Government officers appointed to assist voters in registration and ensuring correct voter list management.
  • Electoral Literacy Clubs (ELCs): Programs initiated by the Election Commission of India to enhance electoral literacy and awareness.

Common Voting Issues

  • Incorrect Voter List: Issues related to missing names or incorrect details in the electoral rolls.
  • Voter ID Discrepancies: Problems regarding name spelling, photo mismatches, and other errors on voter IDs.
  • Non-availability of Polling Stations: Certain areas may lack accessible polling stations for voters, especially in remote areas.
  • Voting Booth Malfunctions: Issues related to electronic voting machines (EVMs) and other booth-related malfunctions.
  • Voter Intimidation: Instances where voters face threats, pressure, or intimidation to vote for a particular candidate or party.

Helplines & Resources for Voting Rights

  • National Voter Helpline: Visit NVSP Portal
  • Voter Assistance Helpline (1950): A toll-free helpline for voters to resolve issues regarding registration, voter IDs, and elections.
  • Election Commission of India (ECI): Visit ECI Website
  • State Election Commission (State-wise Helplines): Available for resolving election-related concerns at the state level.

Important Court Judgments

  • Indira Gandhi vs. Raj Narain (1975): A landmark case concerning the validity of election results and the scope of judicial review in election matters.
  • K. Prabhakaran vs. The Returning Officer (2005): Supreme Court ruling on the invalidation of votes cast by non-eligible persons and voters.
